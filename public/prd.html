PRD: H-1B Bulk Registration Automation

An execution-ready product plan for an attorney-supervised workflow that helps Alma prepare, validate, deduplicate, batch, review, route, and track employer-sponsored H-1B registrations — while preserving human control over legal judgment, client approval, payment, and final USCIS submission.

Primary UserAlma legal ops + attorneys
CustomerEmployer clients filing via legal representative flow
Product ScopePre-USCIS prep, guided filing, and post-filing tracking
PRD FocusReduce legal ops effort without replacing attorney control
EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Product thesis

What Alma should build

An attorney-supervised H-1B cap registration operating layer that sits before and after USCIS: it structures source data, generates the USCIS bulk template, catches errors, flags duplicates, organizes batches, prioritizes attorney review, tracks employer approval, and records final confirmations.

What Alma should not build in MVP

No full autonomous filing bot. In MVP, USCIS login, G-28 completion/signature, employer approval, payment authorization, and final submit remain human-controlled. Alma assists those steps with checklists, prefill, and status tracking.

PAIN POINTS

Where the workflow breaks today

1. Messy source data

Employer lists, beneficiary details, passport data, job details, and petitioner information arrive from different sources and are often incomplete.

2. Repetitive data movement

Legal ops repeatedly maps the same data into templates, USCIS drafts, trackers, approval notes, and confirmation records.

3. High-cost errors

Invalid countries, missing wage levels, wrong petitioner details, and duplicate registrations can create rework or filing risk.

4. Poor batch visibility

Once USCIS splits uploads into batches, teams need to track review, G-28, client approval, payment, submission, and confirmation across each batch.

AUTOMATION STRATEGY

Decision logic: automate, assist, or keep human-controlled

Automate when the task is rules-based

Use automation for repetitive input, data mapping, validation, duplicate detection, batch reconciliation, reminders, confirmation capture, and audit logging.

Assist when judgment is needed

Use AI/workflow support to summarize, prefill, and flag issues, but require human confirmation for petitioner, SOC/OEWS, G-28, and review decisions.

Keep manual when authority matters

Keep USCIS login, client approval, legal certification, payment authorization, and final submit human-controlled because they carry legal or financial responsibility.
